The Biography of Pastry Chef Danie Carr-Morales

Landing Home About PastryPortfolio
The year is 1998 and the day is June 21st, Steven and Deborah Carr welcome the birth of their second child. Two years earlier they were blessed the birth of Danielle’s, Danie for short, older brother Matthew. They grew up in the outskirts of Foxboro, Massachusetts, in a small, yet lively town, called Norfolk. To give you better perspective, the town is a 10-minute drive Northwest of the famous Gillette Stadium, home to the NFL’s New England Patriots. Danie’s father, a Public Account, owns his own business, and mother, is personal secretary to Norfolk’s Police sheriff, ran a respectable and loving household. Danie excelled in multiple sports throughout her adolescence and considers herself more of a “Tom Boy” than a “Barbie Doll” type female.

Throughout her upbringing she developed a close relationship with her father due to their mutual love within sport. Because of this love, Danie’s father would be her brothers’ and her sports coach for most of their youth years. Danie took this passion for playing sports into other parts of her live. She thrived off the competitive nature and the feeling of excelling. This drive for purity and excellence sparked an enjoyment in imagery and discovery. Danie at one time within her high school career even want to go into Criminal Profiling. Life had a different journey though; a tour of a neighboring high school gave her access to witness a young lady constructing a Bird of Paradise design out of apple. At that moment, she fell in love with the creativity and art involved in the undertaking and a career among Law Enforcement was only a thought. After High School Danie went to Johnson and Wales, one of two top colleges known for Pastry Arts on a partial scholarship, to receive her bachelor’s degree. Though she had a full scholarship to venture into the Law, the Pastry World won out. She then stared out her next career journey.

The quest brought her to a paid internship her last year of college with Walt Disney World, in Central Florida. She completed a 6-month program and sought to return in whichever way she could. That way was to apply for another program though she had already graduated and by the blessing eventually led to a full-time position. Danie worked her way and reputation from a position at the Boardwalk resort to eventually, the infamous, Grand Floridian resort. There, she worked under the tutelage of Party Arts Director and Executive Head Chef, Erich Erbicheck. Through dealing with life-long dietary restrictions Danie has still solved her way to reaching the next level of the Pastry World at its Sous Chef Position and known to be one of the youngest to reach that position at Walt Disney World. This love for the art propels her to the continue feeling of having someone try her desert and stating, “I can’t get enough of this, it tastes so good!” Ultimately her goal is to one day rise to level of Head Pastry Chef for one of the newest, innovative, resort openings that happen around the Walt Disney World Property.
